### What is chemistry?
**Chemistry** is the branch of science that deals with composition, properties and behaviour of matter; investigates the reactions of matter and uses these reactions to form new substances.
A person who does research in the field of chemistry is called a **chemist**.

Man has always been curious about nature and various natural phenomena occurring around him. This led to the generation of various ideas, concepts and discoveries. However, the emergence of science dates back around 3000 to 1200 BCE in ancient Egypt and Mesopotamia.

The use of stone tools, using fire for cooking, invention of wheels, growing food (farming), etc. brought massive changes in the life of man. Also, the scientific knowledge gained by various civilisations was passed on to next generations, which led to the development of science and technology.

**Science** deals with the systematic study of the structure and behaviour of physical and natural world through observation and experiment. The three major branches of science are — chemistry, physics (the study of properties of matter and energy) and biology (study of living things—plants and animals).

### DEVELOPMENT OF CHEMISTRY — KNOWING THE PAST
Chemistry is known since the prehistoric times be it the brewers and winemakers using fermentation techniques to make beer, wine and cheese or ancient era housewives leaching the lye from wood ash to make soap or the smiths who learned to combine copper and tin to make bronze.

One of the remarkable uses and study of chemistry was conducted by alchemists in Europe. **Alchemists** primarily aimed to transform common metals into gold or silver using the Philosopher’s stone (the Stone of Knowledge) and developing the elixir of life that could cure any disease and defy death.

---

> **FUN FACT**
>
> The word Alchemy is derived from an Arabic word ‘al-kimia’ which means the art of transmutation of metals. Jabir ibn Hayyan popularly known as Geber in Europe was an Islamic scholar who is known as the Father of Alchemy. People studying alchemy were called alchemists. They were considered the early chemists. Alchemy slowly evolved into chemistry in the 17th century where experiments were given more importance rather than on spiritual thinking.

Although such middle-aged chemists were not fully successful, they made significant discoveries in their attempts. Ancient smiths used naturally occurring and relatively pure metals such as gold, silver and copper.

- Gold was primarily used for jewellery as it was shiny and lustrous. Silver was very uncommon and used much less than gold for similar purposes.
- Copper was used for tools as it could be hardened by hammering but had little value for weapons.
- Additionally, tin was one of the earliest known metals. It was used with copper to form bronze. In contrast to copper, bronze has significant strength and was used for making tools and weapons.

The development of chemistry was primarily concerned with the earliest practical knowledge of metallurgy, pottery and dyes. These techniques were developed with considerable skill but without the knowledge of principles involved.

Chemistry has offered us a lot of things that are vital to our daily life. Sugar, oil, cosmetics, toothpaste, LPG, detergents, soaps, paper, glass, dyes, etc. are some of the gifts of chemistry to mankind. Let us know some applications of chemistry in detail.

#### Agriculture

**Fertilisers:** In order to increase crop yield and refill nutrients, most soil types require the use of fertilisers. Chemists test the soil and recommend which fertiliser type is the most suitable. Some important fertilisers include sodium nitrate, superphosphate, ammonium sulphate, urea and potash. The use of such fertilisers has greatly increased the food output to meet the demands of the growing population. An important example of the use of fertilisers in our country’s history is the ‘Green Revolution’.

**Pesticides:** *Chemical substances that are used to kill pests in crops are called pesticides.* Some examples of pesticides are malathion, parathion, aldrin, etc.

**Insecticides:** *Chemical substances used for killing insects are called insecticides.* For example, DDT (dichlorodiphenyl trichloroethane). In addition to insecticides and pesticides, there are some chemicals that can kill fungi. These are called **fungicides**. Bordeaux mixture, lime sulphur, zinc phosphide, etc., are some fungicides.

#### Health and Sanitation

**Medicines:** Chemistry has enabled us to prepare various drugs and medicines. These medicines have controlled many diseases and saved millions of lives. Surgeries are made painless with the help of anaesthetics.

**Cleaning agents:** The knowledge of chemistry has also enabled us to make various sanitation products. A variety of floor cleaning agents, toilet cleaners, disinfectants, deodorants, air fresheners and antiseptics are available in the market today.

#### Food Processing and Preservation

Chemistry has provided us with technology to process and preserve food. Today we have a wide variety of ready-to-eat food products in the market like cheese, curd, butter, vegetable ghee, refined oil, baby food, milk powder, etc.

Some food items are added with some chemical agents, called **preservatives**, to prevent spoilage by microorganisms, thus increasing their shelf life. Preservatives also restrict the undesirable chemical changes that may occur in the food items (that spoil them). Sugar, common salt, sodium benzoate and sodium metabisulphite are some food preservatives.

Besides preservatives, there is another category of chemical agents called **food additives**.
- They are commonly used to enhance the taste, colour or texture of food.
- They also maintain the freshness and nutritional quality of the food.
- Monosodium Glutamate (MSG), artificial food colouring and sodium nitrite are some examples of food additives.
- Tea, jam, sauce, pickle, etc. are some common food products in which food additives are used.

#### Recreation
Metals, plastics or films used for making pen drives, SD cards, iPods, etc. involve a great deal of chemistry. These items enable us to store variety of data, like music, videos and movies. Moreover, photographic films and various adhesives incorporate the use of chemistry.

#### Cosmetics
Cosmetics are mainly the chemical products used to cleanse, moisturise, protect and change the appearance of external body parts.
- The chief ingredients in most cosmetics include water, emulsifiers, preservatives, thickeners, moisturisers, colours and fragrances. These ingredients may occur naturally or made artificially.
- The potential impact of cosmetics on our health depends on the chemical compounds they are made of. Talcum powder or Talc is the most used cosmetic among all the age groups. Chemically, it is a clay mineral composed of hydrated magnesium silicate.

#### Better Clothing Materials
The knowledge of chemistry has enabled us to make better and more clothing materials. Some wonderful gifts of chemistry include bleached, dyed and printed natural fibres (like cotton, wool, silk, etc.), synthetic fibres (like rayon, nylon, decron, orlon, terylene) and flame-proof textiles.

#### Better Homes
Chemistry has given us a lot of materials for constructing durable homes. Cement, iron, steel, glass, plywood, wires and cables, putty, varnishes and paints are some valuable contributions of chemistry to the mankind.

#### Transport
Knowledge of chemistry is helpful in transport as well. Manufacturing of different automobiles such as aeroplanes, ships, buses, cars, motorcycles and scooters incorporate the knowledge of chemistry and technology. Various chemical reactions are involved in the burning of fuels that produce energy to run automobiles.

#### Communication
Smartphones, telephones, television, audio and video equipment are used in communication purposes. These are made from different metals, silicon chips, plastics, enamel, PVC, etc., which are the gifts of chemistry to the mankind.

### HARMFUL EFFECTS OF CHEMISTRY

Overuse of chemistry has led to adverse effects on humans and the environment.

- The release of smoke and harmful gases from factories and industries into the air causes air pollution. The toxic chemical wastes from factories and industries, when released into waterbodies, cause water pollution.
- Exposure to certain chemicals can pose health risks to humans and other organisms, leading to various health and safety concerns.
- Drug abuse is another negative aspect of chemistry. Some corrupt people produce and sell certain drugs to gain huge profits. These drugs are addictive and can cause serious health problems. Drug addiction is a major problem in our society, especially among young people.
- Development of some chemical weapons like RDX (Royal Demolition Explosive), TNT (trinitrotoluene), hydrogen bomb and atomic bomb if misused can lead to massive destruction of humankind.
