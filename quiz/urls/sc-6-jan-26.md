# What type of rock is formed when magma cools and solidifies?
### Igneous rock
Igneous rocks form when molten rock, called magma, cools and solidifies. If magma cools beneath the Earth's surface, it forms intrusive igneous rocks like granite. If it cools after erupting from a volcano, it forms extrusive rocks like basalt. These rocks are the first to form in the rock cycle and are known as the ‘mother rocks’ of Earth’s crust.
# Which component of soil holds the most water?
### Clay
Clay particles are very small and packed closely together, allowing them to hold a large amount of water. Although clay retains moisture well, it drains poorly, which can make it heavy and sticky when wet. This property makes clay soils suitable for crops like rice but unsuitable for plants that require good drainage like cacti.
# Which layer of soil is rich in humus?
### Topsoil
Topsoil is the uppermost layer of soil and contains humus, which is made of decayed leaves, plants, and animal matter. Humus makes the soil fertile by adding nutrients and improving its ability to hold water. Most plant roots grow in this layer because it is rich in minerals, moisture, and microorganisms that support plant life.
# Which of these is an example of sedimentary rock?
### Limestone
Limestone is a sedimentary rock formed from layers of shells, corals, and tiny marine organisms that settle at the bottom of oceans over millions of years. The weight of layers compresses these materials into solid rock. Sedimentary rocks often contain fossils and are usually softer than igneous or metamorphic rocks.
# What is the process of breaking down rocks into smaller pieces called?
### Weathering
Weathering is the process where rocks break down into smaller pieces due to natural forces like wind, rain, temperature changes, and plant roots. It is the first step in soil formation. Over time, these small rock particles mix with decayed organic matter to form fertile soil, which supports plant life on Earth.
# Which soil is best for growing cotton?

It forms from volcanic rocks and is mainly found in regions like Maharashtra and Gujarat. The soil’s dark color comes from high amounts of organic matter and iron compounds.
# What is the main agent of soil erosion?
### Wind and water
Wind and water are the main agents of soil erosion. Flowing water washes away the top fertile layer of soil, while strong winds blow away dry soil particles. This reduces the soil’s fertility and makes it difficult for crops to grow. Preventing erosion through planting trees and using contour ploughing helps protect farmland.
# Which of the following helps to prevent soil erosion?
### Planting trees
Planting trees helps prevent soil erosion because tree roots hold the soil tightly in place. The leaves also reduce the impact of raindrops on the soil surface, preventing it from being washed away. Forests and vegetation act as natural barriers that protect the land and keep the environment stable.
# Which rock changes into marble after heat and pressure?
### Limestone
When limestone is exposed to intense heat and pressure deep inside the Earth, it transforms into marble. This change happens without melting and is called metamorphism. Marble is a metamorphic rock known for its beauty and is used in building materials and sculptures. The Taj Mahal, for example, is made of white marble.

# Which of the following is a metamorphic rock?
### Marble

# Which phenomenon causes the apparent change in frequency of sound as a source moves relative to an observer?

The Doppler Effect is the change in frequency or pitch of a wave (sound or light) perceived by an observer when the source and observer are moving relative to each other. # Why does an iron ship float on water even though iron sinks?
### Air inside the ship reduces density
An iron ship floats because it is hollow and contains a large amount of air. This air increases the overall volume of the ship without increasing much mass, reducing its average density. When the ship’s average density becomes less than that of water, it displaces enough water to produce an upward buoyant force greater than its weight, allowing it to float.
# How does the structure of alveoli help in efficient gas exchange?
### They are thin and have large surface area
Alveoli are tiny air sacs in the lungs with extremely thin walls and a very large combined surface area. This structure allows oxygen to pass quickly into the blood and carbon dioxide to move out efficiently. The thin walls reduce diffusion distance, while the rich network of blood capillaries ensures rapid transport of gases throughout the body.

# Why does sound travel faster in solids than in gases?

Sound travels through vibrations of particles. In solids, particles are closely packed and strongly bonded, allowing vibrations to pass quickly from one particle to another. In gases, particles are far apart, so vibrations transfer more slowly. This difference in particle arrangement explains why sound travels fastest in solids.

# What is the main function of the skeletal system?
### To support and protect the body
The skeletal system provides shape and support to the human body. It protects important organs such as the brain (protected by the skull) and the heart and lungs (protected by the rib cage). It also works with muscles to help in movement. Without bones, our bodies would not be able to stand upright or perform coordinated physical actions.
# Which part of the brain controls balance and coordination?
### Cerebellum

When you walk, run, or ride a bicycle, the cerebellum ensures your movements are smooth and well-balanced. Damage to this area can cause difficulty in maintaining balance and performing fine movements.
# Which sense organ helps us to maintain body balance?
### Ears
Our ears do more than help us hear — the inner ear contains semicircular canals filled with fluid that help maintain balance. When we move our head, the fluid moves and sends signals to the brain to adjust our posture. This is why we feel dizzy if our inner ear is affected by an infection or sudden movement.
# What are the tiny air sacs in the lungs called?
### Alveoli
Alveoli are small, balloon-like air sacs present in the lungs. They allow the exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide between the air we breathe and our blood. The oxygen enters the blood while carbon dioxide leaves it to be exhaled. Millions of alveoli make the lungs efficient at exchanging gases and supplying oxygen to the whole body.

# Which vitamin is produced when our skin is exposed to sunlight?
### Vitamin D

# Which disease is caused by the deficiency of iodine?
### Goitre
Goitre is caused by a lack of iodine in the diet. Iodine is essential for the thyroid gland to produce hormones that regulate body functions. When iodine is insufficient, the thyroid gland enlarges, causing swelling in the neck. Iodized salt is a simple and effective way to prevent goitre and maintain thyroid health.
# What should be done first when someone gets a minor burn?
### Pour cold water

Ointments or creams should only be applied after cooling. Never use ice or apply oil as it can worsen the injury or trap heat in the skin.

# What is the main source of energy for the water cycle?
### The Sun

# What causes the phases of the Moon?
### Moon’s revolution around Earth
The different shapes of the Moon we see, called phases, are caused by the Moon’s revolution around the Earth. As the Moon moves, sunlight falls on different parts of its surface visible from Earth. This creates phases such as new moon, first quarter, full moon, and last quarter, which repeat every 29.5 days.
# Why can’t we hear sounds on the Moon?
### The Moon has no air
Sound needs a medium like air, water, or solids to travel. Since the Moon has no atmosphere or air, sound waves cannot travel there. This means astronauts on the Moon can only communicate using radios that transmit signals through electromagnetic waves instead of sound. The absence of air also causes extreme temperatures on the Moon.
# What causes an earthquake?
### Movement of tectonic plates
Earthquakes occur when tectonic plates beneath the Earth's crust move suddenly and release energy. These movements cause vibrations that travel through the ground as seismic waves. When the plates rub or collide, the ground shakes, leading to earthquakes. They can cause damage to buildings, roads, and even trigger tsunamis if occurring under the ocean.

Floods are common in regions with poor drainage or near large rivers. Building dams, planting trees, and maintaining proper drainage systems are effective ways to prevent or reduce flood damage.

# What are rocks mainly made up of?
### Minerals

Different combinations of minerals form various types of rocks, such as granite (made of quartz, feldspar, and mica).
# What is the process that turns sediments into sedimentary rocks?
### Compaction and cementation
Sedimentary rocks form through the processes of compaction and cementation. Layers of sediments like sand, clay, and pebbles are pressed together under pressure over millions of years. Natural minerals act like glue to bind these layers, forming rocks such as sandstone and shale.
# Which of the following is an example of an intrusive igneous rock?
### Granite
Granite forms deep within the Earth's crust when magma cools slowly, allowing large crystals to develop. It is an intrusive igneous rock known for its hardness and durability. Granite is widely used in construction, flooring, and monuments due to its attractive appearance.
# Why do sedimentary rocks often contain fossils?
### They form from layers of sediments where remains get trapped
Sedimentary rocks form from the accumulation of layers of sediments like mud, sand, and remains of living organisms. These layers trap and preserve plants and animals before turning into rock. This is why fossils are often found in sedimentary rocks such as limestone and shale.
# What causes metamorphic rocks to form?
### Heat and pressure
Metamorphic rocks form when existing rocks (igneous or sedimentary) undergo changes due to intense heat and pressure deep within the Earth. These conditions alter the rock’s structure and minerals without melting it, forming new rocks like marble from limestone or slate from shale.
# Which rock changes into marble under heat and pressure?
### Limestone
When limestone is subjected to high heat and pressure over long periods, it undergoes metamorphism to become marble. This process changes the structure and texture of the rock, making it harder and more crystalline. Marble is valued for its beauty and used in sculpture and architecture.
# What is magma called when it reaches the Earth’s surface?
### Lava
When molten rock (magma) from beneath the Earth’s crust erupts through volcanoes, it is called lava. Once lava cools and solidifies on the surface, it forms extrusive igneous rocks such as basalt or pumice. This process is part of the continuous rock cycle of the Earth.

The word 'Atom' comes from the Greek word 'atomos,' meaning 'indivisible.' It was first used by ancient Greek philosophers like Democritus, who believed that all matter was made of tiny, indivisible particles.

# What do living things need to stay alive?
### Air, water, and food
All living things need air, water, and food to survive. Air helps in respiration, water is needed for various body functions, and food provides the energy required for growth and daily activities.
# What is the main function of leaves in a plant?
### Make food
Leaves are known as the food factories of plants. They contain chlorophyll, which helps in making food through photosynthesis using sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water.
# Which animals lay eggs?
### Birds
Birds are egg-laying animals. They lay eggs in nests and take care of them until they hatch. The young birds grow inside the egg until they are ready to come out.
# Which part of the plant carries water from roots to other parts?
### Stem
The stem acts like a transport system for the plant. It carries water and nutrients from the roots to leaves and flowers, and food made in leaves to other parts of the plant.
# What are animals that eat both plants and meat called?
### Omnivores
Omnivores are animals that eat both plants and meat. Humans, bears, and pigs are examples of omnivores as they eat a wide range of plant and animal foods.
# What is the main function of muscles?
### Help in movement
Muscles are attached to bones and help us move different parts of our body. They work by contracting and relaxing, allowing activities like walking, running, and lifting.
# Which organ controls all activities in our body?
### Brain
The brain is the control center of our body. It sends messages to all body parts through nerves, guiding our actions, thoughts, and responses to the environment.
# Which disease is caused by a deficiency of vitamin D?
### Rickets
Rickets is caused by a lack of vitamin D, leading to weak and bent bones in children. Sunlight is a good source of vitamin D, along with foods like milk and eggs.
# What should be the first thing done if someone’s clothes catch fire?
### Stop, drop, and roll
When clothes catch fire, the person should stop, drop to the ground, and roll. This action helps cut off oxygen and put out the fire safely without spreading flames.
# Which layer of soil is richest in humus?
### Topsoil
Topsoil is the uppermost layer of soil rich in humus and nutrients. It supports plant growth by holding water and providing essential minerals for roots.
# What is the main cause of air pollution?
### Burning fuels
Burning fuels like coal, petrol, and diesel releases harmful gases that cause air pollution. It can lead to health problems and contribute to global warming.
# Which celestial body reflects sunlight and appears bright in the night sky?
### Moon
The Moon does not have its own light. It reflects sunlight and appears bright at night. Its changing shape is due to different positions relative to the Earth and Sun.
# Which simple machine helps us lift heavy loads with less effort?
### Pulley
A pulley helps lift heavy objects easily by changing the direction of the applied force. It is commonly used in wells and cranes to lift loads vertically.
# What do we call the blanket of air surrounding Earth?
### Atmosphere
The atmosphere is a layer of gases surrounding the Earth. It protects life by filtering harmful solar radiation and maintaining a suitable temperature for living beings.
# Which process turns water into vapor?
### Evaporation
Evaporation is the process by which water changes into vapor due to heat from the sun. This process is part of the water cycle and helps form clouds.
# Which phase of the Moon is seen when it appears completely bright?
### Full Moon
A Full Moon occurs when the Earth is between the Sun and the Moon, and the entire face of the Moon visible from Earth is fully illuminated by sunlight.
# What type of natural disaster is caused by shaking of the Earth’s surface?
### Earthquake
An earthquake is caused by the movement of rocks deep inside the Earth’s crust. It releases energy that shakes the ground, sometimes causing damage to buildings and roads.
# Which resource can be used again and again without running out?
### Wind
Wind is a renewable energy resource that never runs out. It is used to generate electricity through wind turbines and is a clean source of energy.
# Which system of our body helps us breathe?
### Respiratory system
The respiratory system, including the nose, windpipe, and lungs, helps us take in oxygen and release carbon dioxide. This exchange of gases keeps us alive and energetic.
# What should we do to keep our environment clean?
### Plant trees
Planting trees improves air quality, reduces pollution, and helps maintain balance in nature. Trees absorb carbon dioxide and provide oxygen essential for life.
# Why is the topmost layer of soil important?
### It contains organic matter and nutrients
The topmost layer of soil includes humus and topsoil, both rich in organic matter, nutrients, and microorganisms essential for plant growth. These layers are fertile and support life, unlike deeper layers that are infertile and rocky.
# Which human activity is the single main cause of soil erosion?
### Deforestation
Deforestation leads to the removal of trees whose roots bind and hold the soil together. Without these roots, the soil becomes loose and vulnerable to being washed or blown away by rain and wind, causing extensive soil erosion.
# How does water cause soil erosion?
### By washing away the topsoil
Flowing water from rainfall, floods, or irrigation carries away fine soil particles, especially on slopes. When rainwater hits the ground, it dislodges and transports topsoil, which is the most fertile and vital for plant life.
# What happens on slopes during heavy rainfall?
### Topsoil moves downward with water
When rainwater flows down slopes, it carries humus and topsoil along with it. This process is a major cause of erosion in hilly areas, where fertile soil is lost quickly due to the combined action of gravity and water flow.
# How do rivers cause soil erosion?
### By eroding their banks
Fast-moving rivers erode the banks and carry away soil and sediment downstream. This not only changes river paths but also destroys fertile farmland and vegetation near the banks, increasing the loss of soil.
# In which type of area is wind erosion most common?
### Dry, sandy, and barren areas
Wind erosion occurs when strong winds blow over dry, loose, and unprotected soil surfaces. Such conditions are common in deserts and drought-prone regions where vegetation is sparse, allowing wind to carry away topsoil.
# How do fertilizers contribute to soil erosion?
### They make soil loose and prone to washing away
Excessive use of fertilizers can destroy soil structure by breaking down organic matter. This loosens soil particles, which can then be easily washed away by water or blown away by wind, contributing to erosion.
# What is acid rain and how does it affect soil?
### Rain containing pollutants that kill plants and cause soil erosion
Acid rain forms when gases like sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides mix with rainwater. This acidic rain damages vegetation, making the land barren. Without plants to hold soil, erosion increases dramatically.
# What is the main preventive method for soil erosion on barren land?
### Afforestation
Planting trees (afforestation) helps prevent soil erosion as tree roots hold soil particles together. Vegetation also reduces the impact of rainfall and wind, preventing the topsoil from being washed or blown away.
# What is terrace farming also known as?
### Step cultivation
Terrace farming, or step cultivation, involves cutting slopes into flat steps. These terraces slow down water runoff, reducing soil erosion on hillsides. It is commonly used in hilly regions to grow crops like rice.
# What is the purpose of building walls along riverbanks?
### To prevent soil erosion by slowing water flow
Walls, also known as embankments or 'bunds,' are built along rivers to prevent the banks from being washed away. They act as barriers that reduce the speed of flowing water, minimizing erosion of riverbank soil.
# What are check dams used for?
### To slow down the flow of water and prevent erosion
Check dams are small structures built across streams or slopes to slow the movement of water. By reducing flow speed, they prevent soil from being carried away and help recharge groundwater in nearby areas.
# How do windbreakers help control soil erosion?
### They slow down strong winds using trees
Windbreakers are rows of trees planted along fields to reduce wind speed. By slowing the wind, they prevent it from blowing away loose soil particles, thus reducing erosion in dry and windy regions.
# What two main processes shape the Earth's surface using water?
### Weathering and erosion
Water shapes the Earth's surface through weathering and erosion. Weathering breaks down rocks, while erosion transports the broken particles. Over time, these processes form valleys, canyons, cliffs, and coastlines.
# What is mechanical weathering?
### Breaking of rocks by physical forces like rain or temperature
Mechanical weathering is the physical breakdown of rocks without changing their chemical composition. For example, raindrops, ice, or temperature changes can break rocks into smaller particles that contribute to soil formation.
# What forms when water erodes the outer bends of meandering rivers?
### Oxbow lakes
When a river bends, water flows faster on the outer side, eroding the bank, while the inner side deposits sediment. Over time, this creates a horseshoe-shaped lake called an Oxbow lake, formed through erosion and deposition.
# Which type of rock is most easily eroded by carbonic acid in rainwater?
### Limestone
Limestone reacts easily with carbonic acid in rainwater, formed when carbon dioxide dissolves in rain. This causes chemical weathering and forms caves and sinkholes, showing limestone's vulnerability to erosion.
# What role do earthworms play in soil health?
### They loosen soil and improve fertility
Earthworms burrow through soil, mixing organic matter and improving air and water flow. Their activity helps decompose organic material, enriches the soil with nutrients, and makes it more fertile for plant growth.
# Why should we protect soil from pollution and construction?
### Because polluted or sealed soil cannot support life
When soil is covered with concrete or polluted with chemicals, it loses its ability to absorb water and support living organisms. This leads to poor vegetation growth, reduced biodiversity, and long-term environmental damage.
# Which process in plants releases oxygen into the air?
### Photosynthesis
During photosynthesis, green plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce food (glucose) and release oxygen as a by-product. This oxygen is vital for the survival of all living beings on Earth.
# What kind of rock is formed from layers of sand and mud pressed together?
### Sedimentary rock
Sedimentary rocks form when layers of sand, silt, clay, or dead organisms accumulate and are pressed together over millions of years. Examples include sandstone and limestone.
# Which part of the body helps us think and learn?
### Brain
The brain is the control center of the body. It helps us think, learn, remember, and make decisions. It also controls movement, speech, and emotions through its complex network of neurons.
# Which part of a plant anchors it to the ground?
### Root
Roots hold the plant firmly in the soil and absorb water and minerals necessary for growth. Some roots also store food, as seen in carrots and beetroots.
# Which of the following helps in reducing soil erosion?
### Planting trees
Tree roots hold soil tightly and prevent it from being carried away by wind or rain. Planting more trees is one of the best ways to conserve soil and prevent erosion.
# What do we call the movement of animals from one place to another in search of food or to escape cold?
### Migration
Migration is the seasonal movement of animals such as birds, fish, and insects. They migrate to find food, suitable climate, or breeding grounds, returning when conditions improve.
# Which nutrient helps build muscles in our body?
### Proteins
Proteins are the body-building nutrients that help in growth, repair of tissues, and development of muscles. Foods like eggs, milk, and pulses are rich in proteins.
# Which simple machine is found in scissors?
### Lever
Scissors use two levers joined at a pivot point to cut objects. The handle acts as the effort arm, while the blades apply the load, making it easier to cut with less force.
# What causes day and night on Earth?
### Rotation
Earth’s rotation on its axis causes day and night. As Earth spins, different parts face the Sun, resulting in daytime, while the other side experiences darkness, leading to nighttime.
# What is the main cause of droughts?
### No rainfall for a long time
A drought occurs when an area receives little or no rainfall for a long period, leading to dry soil, crop failure, and water shortages for people and animals.
# Which disease is caused by lack of vitamin C?
### Scurvy
Scurvy is a disease caused by a deficiency of vitamin C. It leads to bleeding gums, weakness, and slow healing of wounds. Eating citrus fruits like oranges and lemons prevents scurvy.
# What is the function of the spinal cord?
### Carries messages to and from the brain
The spinal cord acts as a communication path between the brain and the rest of the body. It carries signals that control movement and reflexes, making quick responses possible.
# Which of these is a renewable natural resource?
### Sunlight
Sunlight is renewable because it is continuously available and cannot be exhausted by use. Unlike coal or petroleum, it is a clean source of energy that supports life on Earth.
# What kind of rock forms under heat and pressure?
### Metamorphic rock
Metamorphic rocks form when existing rocks change due to extreme heat and pressure. Examples include marble (from limestone) and slate (from shale).
# Which gas is needed for burning to take place?
### Oxygen
Oxygen supports combustion, allowing substances like wood or gas to burn. Without oxygen, fire cannot exist, which is why covering a flame removes air and puts it out.
# What is the main cause of floods?
### Heavy rainfall
Floods occur when heavy rainfall causes rivers, lakes, or seas to overflow, submerging nearby areas. Poor drainage and deforestation can make flooding worse.
# Which natural satellite helps control tides on Earth?
### Moon
The Moon’s gravity pulls on Earth's oceans, creating tides. When the Moon and Sun align, tides are higher, and when they are at right angles, tides are lower.
# Which of the following is a Rabi crop?
### Wheat
Rabi crops are sown in winter (October–December) and harvested in spring (March–April). Wheat grows well in cool climates and is mainly cultivated in northern India. It requires less rainfall and more irrigation compared to Kharif crops. On the other hand, rice and maize are Kharif crops that need heavy rainfall and warm temperatures.
# Kharif crops are mainly sown during which season?
### Monsoon
Kharif crops are sown at the beginning of the monsoon season, usually between June and July. These crops rely heavily on rainfall and warm, humid weather. Farmers plant them when the first rains arrive and harvest them by September or October. Common examples include rice, maize, and cotton.
# Which crop among the following is a Zaid crop?
### Watermelon
Zaid crops are grown in the summer months between the Rabi and Kharif seasons, typically from March to June. Watermelon is a popular Zaid crop because it thrives in warm weather with proper irrigation. These crops are short-duration and provide extra income for farmers during the gap between the main cropping seasons.
# In which months are Rabi crops generally harvested?
### March–April
Rabi crops are sown during the winter months (October–December) and harvested in spring (March–April). This timing allows them to grow in cooler weather and mature when conditions are warm and dry, ideal for harvesting. Crops like wheat, barley, and mustard fall into this category.
# Which climatic condition is most suitable for Kharif crops?
### Hot and humid
Kharif crops require hot and humid conditions with plenty of rainfall. They are typically sown in June or July, when the monsoon begins. This weather supports the growth of crops like rice and maize, which need a large amount of water. Without sufficient rainfall, Kharif crops may not grow properly.
# Zaid crops are grown between which two crop seasons?
### Rabi and Kharif
Zaid crops are grown between the Rabi and Kharif seasons, mainly from March to June. This period falls after the Rabi harvest and before the Kharif sowing begins. Zaid crops, such as cucumber and muskmelon, take advantage of the warm summer climate and are usually grown with irrigation.
# Which of the following pairs is correctly matched?
### Zaid – Watermelon
Watermelon is correctly matched as a Zaid crop, grown during the summer months (March–June). Rice is a Kharif crop, while wheat is a Rabi crop. Cotton is also a Kharif crop. Each crop type is suited to specific weather and water conditions depending on the season.
# Why do Rabi crops require irrigation?
### Because rainfall is less in winter
Rabi crops are grown in the winter season when rainfall is very low. Hence, irrigation is necessary to provide adequate water for their growth. Farmers use canals, wells, or tube wells for watering crops like wheat and mustard. Unlike Kharif crops, which depend on monsoon rains, Rabi crops rely mainly on artificial irrigation.
# Which of these crops grows best during the monsoon season?
### Rice
Rice is a typical Kharif crop that grows best in the monsoon season. It needs a warm climate and standing water during its growing period. States like West Bengal, Odisha, and Assam are major rice producers. In contrast, peas, barley, and mustard are Rabi crops that grow in cooler weather.
# What is a common feature of Zaid crops?
### They are short-duration summer crops
Zaid crops are short-duration crops grown during the summer months (March–June). They are planted after Rabi crops are harvested and before Kharif crops are sown. These crops grow quickly and require warm temperatures and sufficient irrigation. Examples include watermelon, muskmelon, and cucumber.
# What is evaporation?
### Turning of water into water vapour
Evaporation is the process by which liquid water changes into water vapour due to heat. This happens when the Sun heats up water from oceans, rivers, and lakes, causing it to rise into the air. Evaporation is an important part of the water cycle as it helps form clouds.
# Which part of the plant prepares food?
### Leaf
Leaves are called the food factories of plants because they prepare food using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide through the process of photosynthesis. The green pigment chlorophyll helps absorb sunlight to make food for the plant, which is later distributed to other parts.
# What helps blood to clot when we get a cut?
### Platelets
Platelets are small cell fragments in our blood that help it clot when we get injured. When a cut occurs, platelets gather at the wound and form a clot to stop bleeding. This prevents excessive blood loss and protects the body from infection.
# Which gas do plants release during photosynthesis?
### Oxygen
During photosynthesis, plants take in carbon dioxide and water in the presence of sunlight and chlorophyll to produce food. In this process, oxygen is released as a byproduct. This oxygen is essential for animals and humans to breathe and survive.
# What type of animals eat both plants and other animals?
### Omnivores
Omnivores are animals that eat both plants and other animals. Humans, bears, and crows are examples of omnivores. Their digestive systems are adapted to process both plant-based and meat-based food, providing them with a balanced source of nutrients.
# Which organ pumps blood in our body?
### Heart
The heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood throughout the body. It ensures that oxygen and nutrients reach all body parts while waste products like carbon dioxide are carried away. The rhythmic beating of the heart keeps blood flowing continuously.
# Which organ helps in breathing?
### Lungs
Lungs are the main organs of the respiratory system. They help us breathe by taking in oxygen from the air and releasing carbon dioxide from the blood. The exchange of gases occurs in tiny air sacs called alveoli inside the lungs.
# What is the process by which plants lose water from their leaves?
### Transpiration
Transpiration is the process where plants lose water in the form of vapour from their leaves through tiny pores called stomata. This process helps cool the plant and maintains the flow of water and nutrients from roots to leaves.
# Which part of the body helps us to sense sound?
### Ears
Ears help us hear sounds and maintain balance. Sound waves enter the ear and cause vibrations that are converted into signals sent to the brain. The brain then interprets these signals as different sounds, helping us communicate and stay aware of our surroundings.
# What causes day and night?
### Rotation of Earth
Day and night occur because Earth rotates on its axis. The side facing the Sun experiences day, while the opposite side has night. This rotation takes about 24 hours to complete, giving us one full day and night cycle.
# What do we call animals that give birth to young ones?
### Viviparous
Viviparous animals are those that give birth to live young ones instead of laying eggs. Examples include humans, dogs, and cats. The young develop inside the mother’s body and are nourished before birth, ensuring better chances of survival.
# Which organ helps filter waste from our blood?
### Kidney
The kidneys are vital organs that filter waste products and excess water from the blood to form urine. They help maintain the body’s fluid balance and remove toxins, ensuring the internal environment remains clean and healthy.
# What is condensation?
### Change of gas into liquid
Condensation is the process where water vapour in the air cools down and changes back into liquid water. This process helps form clouds and dew. It is the opposite of evaporation and plays a crucial role in the water cycle.
# Which force pulls objects toward the Earth?
### Gravitational force
Gravitational force is the invisible pull that attracts all objects toward the Earth's center. It keeps us and other things grounded and is the reason why objects fall when dropped. Gravity also keeps planets in orbit around the Sun.
# Which celestial body gives light at night by reflecting sunlight?
### Moon
The Moon shines at night not because it produces light, but because it reflects the light of the Sun. It orbits the Earth and appears in different shapes or phases as it moves around, from new moon to full moon.
# What is the natural satellite of Earth?
### Moon
The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. It revolves around the Earth and affects ocean tides through its gravitational pull. The Moon takes about 27 days to complete one revolution around Earth, causing changes in its visible shape.
# Which organ helps in digestion by producing bile?
### Liver
The liver produces bile, a greenish-yellow fluid that helps digest fats. It also helps remove toxins and stores nutrients. The liver is the largest internal organ in the human body and plays a key role in metabolism and energy balance.
