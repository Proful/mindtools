  1. Sources of Light:

There are two kinds of light. One is from nature, and the other is made by us.

  • Natural Light:
    The sun is a big light. It is very bright. Stars also give light. Fire and fireflies are other lights made by nature.

  • Artificial Light:
    Lamps, bulbs, and candles are lights made by people. We use them when it is dark.

  1. Reflection and Refraction:

  • Reflection:
    When light hits a shiny thing, it bounces back. This is called reflection. Look at a mirror. You see your face. That is light bouncing back.

  • Refraction:
    When light passes through glass or water, it bends. This is called refraction. Put a stick in a glass of water. It looks bent. That is refraction.

  1. Transparent, Translucent, and Opaque:

  • Transparent:
    Things like glass let light pass. We can see through them. They are clear.

  • Translucent:
    Some things let some light pass. We can see shapes but not clear things. Thin paper is like this.

  • Opaque:
    Things like wood do not let light pass. We cannot see through them.

  1. Shadows and Their Properties:

When light hits an object, it makes a shadow. Shadows are dark shapes. They are made when light is blocked.

  • Shape:
    The shape of the shadow is like the shape of the thing blocking the light.

  • Size:
    The size of the shadow can change. If the light is close, the shadow is big. If the light is far, the shadow is small.

  • Dark or Light:
    Shadows can be dark or light. If the light is strong, the shadow is dark. If the light is weak, the shadow is light.

Light is fun to learn about. It helps us see, play, and learn every day!
