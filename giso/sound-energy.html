Sources of Sound

Sound comes from things that shake or move back and forth. This is called vibration. When you hit a drum, it shakes. This shaking makes sound. Other sources of sound are:

  • Animals: Birds sing. Dogs bark. Cows moo.
  • People: We talk, clap, and sing.
  • Machines: Cars honk. Phones ring.
  • Nature: Wind blows. Rain falls. Thunder booms.

Pitch and Volume

Pitch tells us if a sound is high or low. Think of a bird chirping. That is a high pitch. A big drum makes a low pitch. The size of the object and how fast it shakes changes the pitch.

Volume tells us how loud or soft a sound is. If you shout, the sound is loud. If you whisper, the sound is soft. Volume depends on the power of the vibration. Strong shakes make loud sounds. Weak shakes make soft sounds.

How Sound Travels

Sound moves in waves. These waves travel through air, water, and solid things. Imagine dropping a rock in water. You see waves. Sound waves move like that. Sound moves fast in air. It moves faster in water. It moves fastest in solid things.

Noise Pollution

Not all sounds are nice. Some sounds are too loud or go on for too long. This is noise pollution. Noise pollution can hurt our ears. It can make us feel stressed. It can even make us sick.

To stop noise pollution, we can:

  • Wear earplugs.
  • Keep the volume low.
  • Stay away from loud places.

Sound is all around us. It helps us talk, play, and learn. But we should also protect our ears from too much noise.
