Work and Energy

What is Work?

Think of work like moving things. When you push, pull, or lift something, you are doing work. Work is when you use your hands, legs, or any part of your body to move something.

For example, when you push a toy car, you are doing work. If you pick up a book, you are also doing work. But if you sit and do nothing, you are not doing any work.

How Do We Measure Work?

Work is measured by how hard you push or pull and how far the object moves.

If you push a toy car very hard and it moves far, you did a lot of work. If you push it lightly and it moves only a little, you did less work.

How is Work Different from Just Moving?

Not all moving is work. If you push on a wall and it does not move, you are not doing work. For work to happen, the thing you push or pull must move.

Force and Motion

Force is a push or pull. Motion is when something moves. When you use force, you make things move.

Imagine you have a ball. If you kick it, you use force, and the ball moves. The harder you kick, the faster the ball goes. The ball moving is motion.

How Do Force and Motion Work Together?

Force and motion are best friends. Force makes things move. Motion shows that the force worked.

If you push a toy car with a lot of force, it will move fast. If you push it softly, it will move slow. The more force, the more motion.

Types of Force

There are many kinds of force. Here are some:

  • Push: Like pushing a door open.
  • Pull: Like pulling a rope.
  • Lift: Like picking up a book.
  • Drop: Like letting go of a toy.

Energy: What Is It?

Energy is what makes things move, glow, or grow. Energy is like the power that makes things work.

There are two main kinds of energy:

  • Renewable energy
  • Non-renewable energy

Renewable Energy

Renewable energy comes from things that never run out. Like:

  • Sun: The sun gives light and heat.
  • Wind: Wind can spin big fans to make power.
  • Water: Rivers and oceans can spin wheels to make power.

Non-Renewable Energy

Non-renewable energy comes from things that can run out. Like:

  • Coal: Black rocks that burn.
  • Oil: Thick black liquid.
  • Gas: Air that burns.

Once these are used up, they are gone forever. We should save them.

Why Save Energy?

When we use less energy, we help the Earth. Using too much non-renewable energy can hurt the air, water, and land. Using more renewable energy is good for nature.

Summary

  • Work is when you move something.
  • Force is a push or pull.
  • Motion is when things move.
  • Energy makes things work.
  • Renewable energy never runs out.
  • Non-renewable energy can run out.
