Motion for Kids

What is Motion?

Motion is when a thing moves from one spot to another. It can go up, down, left, right, fast, or slow. When we walk, run, or ride a bike, we are in motion. When a toy car rolls on the floor, it is in motion too. Everything that moves is in motion.

Why Do Things Move?

Things move because of a push or a pull. A push is when you shove a toy away from you. A pull is when you drag a toy closer to you. Both push and pull make things move.

Types of Motion

There are three main types of motion:

  1. Linear Motion:
    This is when a thing moves in a straight line. Think of a car going down a straight road. It goes in one line. A train on a track also moves in a straight line. When you kick a ball straight, it moves in a linear motion.

  2. Circular Motion:
    This is when a thing moves in a circle. Think of the hands of a clock. They move in a circle. The wheels of a bike also move in circles. Even when we spin a top, it goes in a circle.

  3. Periodic Motion:
    This is when a thing moves back and forth in a set time. A swing at a park goes back and forth in a set way. The hands of a clock go around and around in a set time. The sun and moon move in a set way too. They rise and set each day in a set time.

Speed and Direction

Speed is how fast or slow a thing moves. If a car goes fast, it has more speed. If it goes slow, it has less speed. We can say, "The bike is slow," or "The car is fast."

Direction is the way a thing moves. It can go up, down, left, or right. A ball thrown up moves up. A slide at the park goes down. A car on the road can turn left or right.

What is Velocity?

When we talk about speed and direction together, we call it 'velocity.' If a car goes fast to the north, we say it has high velocity to the north. If it goes slow to the west, it has low velocity to the west.

Why Does Motion Stop?

Motion can stop if there is a force that stops it. If you roll a ball on the ground, it will stop after some time. The ground pushes back on the ball. This is called friction. Friction can stop motion. Another way to stop motion is to pull or push the object in the opposite way.
